The Architectural Framework

Three Pillars.
One Architecture.

Sustainable AI adoption doesn't start with technology. It starts with three architectural decisions that provosts control.

PILLAR 1

Friction Tolerance

Which pedagogical struggles should we preserve, and which should we remove?

Faculty fear that AI will eliminate the intellectual struggle essential to learning. Friction Tolerance gives your institution a principled framework for distinguishing between productive friction (Socratic dialogue, grappling with essential questions) and administrative friction (grading mechanics, scheduling).

Theorists

Freire, Gattegno, Papert, Montessori, Csikszentmihalyi

PILLAR 2

Lateral Bandwidth

How do we assess more while reducing proportional faculty effort?

Lateral Bandwidth means expanding what you assess (diverse modalities, cross-cultural evidence, higher-order thinking) and how often (continuous formative loops) while reducing faculty grading load by 40-60%.

Theorists

Bloom, Wiliam, Vygotsky, Mitra, Wiggins & McTighe

PILLAR 3

Incentive Layer

How do we create conditions where faculty WANT to adopt AI?

Compliance produces theater. Buy-in produces transformation. The Incentive Layer framework redesigns institutional systems so faculty adoption becomes intrinsically motivated by professional purpose, peer belonging, and creative agency.

Theorists

Kotter, Senge, Sinek, Lave & Wenger, Dweck
