The 10 Ancestors

The pioneers whose vision of human potential helps us architect the AI age.

The Architect

Dr. Maria Montessori

Pioneered the 'prepared environment'. In the AI era, she would architect digital spaces where autonomous agents scan for student needs.

The Networker

Dr. Lev Vygotsky

Defined the Zone of Proximal Development. In a world of LLMs, he's the master of the prompt, knowing exactly where the AI should step in.

The Encoder

Dr. Caleb Gattegno

Believed only awareness is educable. He would treat AI not as a knowledge bank, but as a feedback loop—a mirror for learning.

The Liberator

Dr. Paulo Freire

He would weaponize AI literacy to dismantle epistemic privilege, forcing students to question the bias of the algorithm.

The Maker

Dr. Seymour Papert

He would hand students the keys to the LLM, demanding they build their own cognitive tools rather than being programmed by the system.

The Alchemist

Sir Ken Robinson

He would demand we use Generative AI not to standardize testing, but to personalize expression, turning every student into a singular artist.

The Gardener

Dr. Carol Dweck

She would frame AI hallucinations not as failures, but as 'not yet' moments—invitations to debug and refine the mind.

The Stoic

Dr. Angela Duckworth

She would see AI not as a shortcut, but as a sparring partner—a high-friction tool requiring passion and perseverance to master.

The Disruptor

Dr. Sugata Mitra

He would believe that given AI, children would organize their own learning, making traditional curriculum as obsolete as a paper map.

The Optimizer

Dr. Benjamin Bloom

He would see AI tutors as the only scalable way to give every student the 1-on-1 mastery coaching previously reserved for aristocracy.
